Retail / Omnichannel

You're in 200 doors and still can't tell which channel is profitable.

Foot traffic declined across nearly every retail category in 2025 - department stores down 4.6%, cosmetics down 5.3%, toy stores down 16%.

(Source: Foursquare - US Retail Foot Traffic 2025)

Over 8,000 U.S. stores closed in 2025 - a 13.2% increase from 2024. Coresight Research projected roughly 15,000 closures for the full year. Already 1,400+ more planned for 2026.

(Source: IndexBox / Coresight Research; Business Insider)

But the brands that are dying aren't dying because of foot traffic. They're dying because of channel chaos.

Online systems don't talk to store systems. Inventory is synced late - or not at all. You oversell on Shopify while pallets sit untouched in a warehouse. Pricing doesn't match across platforms. Return rate online is 22%. In-store it's 6%. You're managing both with the same team and the same broken process.

When you rushed to go omnichannel during COVID, you duct-taped systems together that were never built to work as one. Now you've got duplicated spend, fragmented customer data, and your own channels cannibalizing each other's margins.

But here's what the closures don't tell you: retail isn't dying - it's evolving. The future of retail is the store as a hub. Community. Human connection. Live shopping straight from your floor - TikTok Shop, emerging live platforms - where customers see real product in a real space. In an AI world where anyone can generate a storefront and pretend to sell, broadcasting live from your own store is proof you can't fake. That's how retail bounces back. Not more doors. Smarter doors.

The brands that solve this will own the next decade. The ones that don't will join the 8,000.
